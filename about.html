About Our Museum

Founded in 1985, the Wings of Hope WWII Aircraft Museum is dedicated to preserving, restoring, and displaying aircraft from the Second World War. Our collection represents one of the most comprehensive assemblies of WWII-era aircraft in the world, with examples from all major combatant nations.

"Our mission is to honor the courage and sacrifice of those who served by preserving these magnificent aircraft and sharing their stories with future generations."

Our History

What began as a small private collection of WWII memorabilia has grown into a world-class aviation museum. Founder Colonel James Henderson (USAF Ret.) started with just one restored P-51 Mustang in a rented hangar. Today, our 150,000 square foot facility houses over 50 fully restored aircraft, with many more in various stages of restoration.

The Collection

Our collection includes rare and historically significant aircraft from all theaters of WWII. Many of our aircraft are the last surviving examples of their type, painstakingly restored to their original wartime configuration. Several are still airworthy and participate in airshows across the country.

Collection Highlights

P-51D Mustang

American Fighter

Our "Old Crow" is one of only three P-51s still flying with its original Merlin engine. This aircraft flew 76 combat missions over Europe.

Spitfire Mk.IX

British Fighter

This Spitfire served with RAF No. 611 Squadron and participated in the D-Day operations. Restored to its 1944 markings.

B-17G Flying Fortress

American Bomber

"Memphis Belle II" is one of only about a dozen airworthy B-17s remaining. Our ground crew maintains it in pristine condition.

Messerschmitt Bf 109

German Fighter

This rare G-6 variant was recovered from a Russian lake in 1991. After a 12-year restoration, it's now one of our most prized exhibits.

Mitsubishi A6M Zero

Japanese Fighter

One of only three original Zeros still flying worldwide. This aircraft was recovered from the Marshall Islands in 1974.

Consolidated PBY Catalina

American Patrol Bomber

Our "Black Cat" Catalina is painted in its distinctive night operations scheme and is fully amphibious.

Education & Preservation

Beyond displaying aircraft, we're committed to educating visitors about WWII aviation history. Our museum offers:

  • Guided tours led by veteran docents
  • Flight demonstrations during summer months
  • Restoration workshops where visitors can watch our team work
  • Oral history recordings from WWII veterans
  • Educational programs for schools and youth groups

We also operate one of the most advanced aircraft restoration facilities in the world, where we bring these historic aircraft back to life using original techniques and materials whenever possible.
